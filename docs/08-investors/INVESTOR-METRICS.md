# Investor Metrics

Defines practical investor metrics: pipeline stage count, investor conversion, diligence cycle time, committed capital, runway, burn, revenue progress, retention, growth, operating milestones, and update consistency. Like the metrics standards elsewhere in Startup OS, these measure the fundraising and reporting *system*, not a single vanity number, and no specific tool is assumed.

## Pipeline Stage Count

Number of investor conversations at each stage of [INVESTOR-PIPELINE.md](INVESTOR-PIPELINE.md) — read for realistic momentum, the same way sales pipeline health is read by stage rather than one blended count.

## Investor Conversion

Rate at which investor conversations progress stage to stage, and ultimately convert to committed capital — a low conversion at a specific stage points to exactly where the pitch or process needs work.

## Diligence Cycle Time

Time from active diligence start to resolution ([DUE-DILIGENCE.md](DUE-DILIGENCE.md)) — a lengthening cycle time often traces to data room gaps or slow internal answer routing, both fixable process issues.

## Committed Capital

Capital actually committed (signed, funded) versus verbally discussed — tracked as distinct categories; committed capital is the only figure that should ever be described as "raised."

## Runway and Burn

Current runway and burn rate, from [FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md](FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md) — tracked over time, not just as a point-in-time model output, since burn patterns shift.

## Revenue Progress

Actual revenue against the model's projected revenue — the most direct check on whether the financial model's assumptions are holding up in reality.

## Retention and Growth

Pulled directly from [CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-METRICS.md](../13-customer-success/CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-METRICS.md) (renewal, churn) and [SALES-METRICS.md](../07-sales/SALES-METRICS.md) (win rate, pipeline growth) — not recalculated independently for investor purposes, so the investor-facing number always matches the operational source of truth.

## Operating Milestones

Progress against the specific milestones named in [FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md](FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md) — the concrete measure of whether the capital raised is actually producing what it was raised to produce.

## Update Consistency

Whether [investor updates](INVESTOR-UPDATES.md) are actually going out on the stated cadence — a process metric on the company's own discipline, not on business performance, but one that materially affects investor trust.

## Using These Metrics

- Review on a regular rhythm, the same discipline as every other metrics standard in Startup OS.
- React to trends, not single data points — one soft month is noise; three months of declining conversion is signal.
- Every metric used externally (in a deck, an update, a diligence answer) is the same number tracked internally — no separate "investor-facing" version that reads more favorably.

## Right-Sizing for Team Size

At pre-seed/seed, with a founder tracking this alone: most of this is a simple tracked list, checked before any investor conversation. The metrics don't change as the company grows, only the rigor and tooling used to track them.
