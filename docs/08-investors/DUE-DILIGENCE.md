# Due Diligence

Defines how investor diligence requests are received, assigned, answered, reviewed, and tracked. No specific diligence or data room tool is assumed.

## Receiving Requests

Every diligence request is logged — what's being asked, by which investor, by when — the same intake discipline [SUPPORT-INTAKE-AND-TRIAGE.md](../09-operations/SUPPORT-INTAKE-AND-TRIAGE.md) applies to support requests, applied here to investor questions.

## Assigning Requests

Routed to whoever actually holds the answer: finance for financial questions, product for roadmap questions, security/compliance for trust and compliance questions ([COMPLIANCE-OBLIGATIONS.md](../14-security-compliance/COMPLIANCE-OBLIGATIONS.md), [VENDOR-AND-THIRD-PARTY-RISK.md](../14-security-compliance/VENDOR-AND-THIRD-PARTY-RISK.md)), legal for corporate/contractual questions. Leadership does not answer everything from memory when a more accurate source exists.

## Answering

Answers are factual and sourced from the same evidence base as [PITCH-NARRATIVE.md](PITCH-NARRATIVE.md) and the [data room](INVESTOR-DATA-ROOM.md) — never a more favorable version constructed for the specific investor asking. A gap or a "we don't have that yet" is answered honestly, the same principle [TRUST-COMMUNICATIONS-AND-CLAIMS.md](../14-security-compliance/TRUST-COMMUNICATIONS-AND-CLAIMS.md) applies to any external claim.

## Review Before Sending

A diligence answer with financial, legal, security, or compliance content is reviewed by whoever holds that expertise before it's sent — the stakes of an inaccurate diligence answer (potential legal exposure, damaged trust, a collapsed deal discovered later) are higher than almost any other external communication the company makes.

## Tracking

Every request and its answer is tracked to completion — an unanswered or partially answered diligence request left open signals disorganization at the worst possible time.

## Escalating

A request the company can't yet answer accurately (missing data, a genuinely unresolved question) is escalated internally rather than answered with a guess — "let us get back to you with an accurate answer" is always better than a wrong one discovered later.

## Required Artifacts

A diligence request log: question, assignee, answer, and status; a record of anything escalated and how it was resolved.

## Ownership

Leadership coordinates the overall diligence process; each function answers within its own expertise; security/compliance and legal review anything in their domain before it's sent.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Guessing under pressure.** Answering a hard question quickly and inaccurately to keep the deal moving.
- **Unrouted requests.** Leadership answering a technical or financial question from memory instead of routing it to the actual source.
- **Untracked requests.** Diligence questions answered informally in conversation with no record, discovered as unresolved later.
