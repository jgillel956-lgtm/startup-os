# Investor Updates

Defines how recurring investor updates are written, reviewed, distributed, archived, and used to maintain trust and momentum. No specific distribution tool is assumed.

## Why Updates Matter

Investors who hear from the company only when it's raising again have no ongoing relationship to draw on — regular updates build the trust and context that make the next raise, or a difficult conversation, easier. This mirrors [RELATIONSHIP-MANAGEMENT.md](../13-customer-success/RELATIONSHIP-MANAGEMENT.md)'s "reinforce value continuously, not just at the moment you need something" principle, applied to investor relationships.

## Cadence

A stated, predictable rhythm (commonly monthly or quarterly) — kept even when the news isn't all positive. A company that only sends updates when things are going well trains investors to read silence as bad news.

## Content

Typically covers: progress against the milestones from [FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md](FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md), key metrics ([INVESTOR-METRICS.md](INVESTOR-METRICS.md)), notable wins and challenges, and specific asks (an introduction, advice, a hire). Content is honest about challenges — the same "communicate uncertainty without overcommitting" discipline as [CUSTOMER-COMMUNICATION-STANDARDS.md](../09-operations/CUSTOMER-COMMUNICATION-STANDARDS.md).

## Writing Standards

Direct and specific — real numbers and real progress, not vague positivity. An update that only ever says "things are going great" with no substantiating detail reads as evasive over time, even when things genuinely are going well.

## Review

Updates containing financial figures, metrics, or claims are checked for accuracy against the actual current model/metrics before sending — the same discipline as any other external communication in this domain.

## Distribution

Sent consistently to the full relevant investor list — not selectively to whoever the founder happens to think of that month, which erodes trust with whoever's left out.

## Archiving

Past updates are kept — they're a running record of the company's actual trajectory, useful both for the company's own reflection and for a future raise's narrative ([PITCH-NARRATIVE.md](PITCH-NARRATIVE.md)).

## Required Artifacts

Each update, archived; a distribution list kept current as investors join.

## Ownership

Leadership writes and owns investor updates; finance confirms figures before distribution.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Good-news-only updates.** Skipping updates or omitting real challenges during a hard quarter.
- **Inconsistent cadence.** Sending updates sporadically, so investors can't rely on hearing from the company regularly.
- **Vague positivity.** Updates with tone but no substantiating detail.
