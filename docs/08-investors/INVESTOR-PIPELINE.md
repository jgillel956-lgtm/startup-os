# Investor Pipeline

Defines how investor targets, introductions, conversations, stages, follow-ups, objections, next steps, and outcomes are tracked. No specific investor CRM is assumed — the discipline mirrors [SALES-PIPELINE.md](../07-sales/SALES-PIPELINE.md)'s deal hygiene, applied to fundraising conversations instead of sales deals.

## Stages

A typical sequence — a specific company may adapt it, but every stage should have a clear entry/exit condition:

| Stage | Exit criteria |
|-------|-----------------|
| Target identified | Fit assessed (stage, sector, check size) |
| Introduction / first contact | A real conversation scheduled |
| Initial meeting | Interest confirmed or declined |
| Deeper diligence | Investor actively evaluating (see [DUE-DILIGENCE.md](DUE-DILIGENCE.md)) |
| Term sheet / negotiation | Terms under discussion |
| Closed (committed) / Passed | Terminal — capital committed, or investor declined |

## Deal Hygiene

Every active investor conversation has a stated next step and realistic next-action date — the same rule [SALES-PIPELINE.md](../07-sales/SALES-PIPELINE.md) applies to sales deals. An investor conversation with no next step for an extended period is stalled, not "still warm."

## Tracking Objections

Reasons an investor passes or hesitates are recorded — market size concern, team gap, traction stage, terms — the same discipline as [OBJECTION-HANDLING.md](../07-sales/OBJECTION-HANDLING.md)'s documentation requirement. A pattern across multiple passes (the same concern repeatedly) is a signal worth addressing in the narrative or the business itself, not just something to work around investor by investor.

## Follow-Ups

Investors who pass or go quiet are followed up deliberately at meaningful milestones (a funding close, a major metric milestone) rather than left cold permanently — many investors reconsider once real progress is visible.

## Outcomes

Every pipeline entry ends in a recorded outcome: committed, passed (with reason), or still active — mirroring [SALES-PIPELINE.md](../07-sales/SALES-PIPELINE.md)'s no-zombie-deals discipline.

## Required Artifacts

A tracked investor list with stage, next step, and outcome per entry; a summarized objection/pass-reason log.

## Ownership

Leadership owns the investor pipeline and every relationship in it — this is not delegated to a general sales function.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Zombie investor conversations.** Contacts left "in progress" indefinitely with no real next step.
- **Losing objection patterns.** Hearing the same concern from multiple investors and never connecting the dots.
- **All-or-nothing follow-up.** Treating a pass as permanent and never re-engaging even after real progress.
