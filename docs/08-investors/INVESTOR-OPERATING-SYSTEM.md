# Investor Operating System

Defines the overall investor operating model: ownership, principles, cadence, artifacts, review expectations, and decision standards. No specific investor CRM, data room tool, or fundraising platform is assumed. This document does not provide legal, tax, accounting, or securities advice.

## Principles

1. **Evidence over narrative polish.** A pitch is compelling because what it claims is true and verifiable, not because it's well-designed — polish supports evidence, it never substitutes for it.
2. **Consistency across audiences.** What's told to an investor matches what's told to customers, employees, and the public — a narrative that only holds up in one room isn't a narrative, it's a risk waiting to surface.
3. **Diligence-ready by default.** Materials and the data room are kept current continuously, not assembled in a scramble once a raise starts — see [INVESTOR-DATA-ROOM.md](INVESTOR-DATA-ROOM.md).
4. **Claims are verified before they're made.** Financial, legal, security, and compliance claims go through the same discipline as [TRUST-COMMUNICATIONS-AND-CLAIMS.md](../14-security-compliance/TRUST-COMMUNICATIONS-AND-CLAIMS.md) — never asserted to make a raise easier.
5. **Lightweight until it needs to not be.** Pre-seed materials can be a simple deck and a spreadsheet; the discipline (accuracy, consistency, readiness) matters more than production value.
6. **The raise serves the strategy, not the reverse.** Fundraising decisions ([FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md](FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md)) trace to [BUSINESS-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md](../01-business/BUSINESS-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md) and [COMPANY-STRATEGY.md](../01-business/COMPANY-STRATEGY.md) — the company doesn't reshape its strategy just to fit what's easiest to pitch.

## Ownership

See the ownership boundary in [README.md](README.md).

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|-----------------|
| Founder / leadership | Fundraising decisions, investor relationships, final claim approval |
| Finance function (or founder, at small scale) | Financial model integrity ([FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md](FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md)) |
| Marketing | Narrative and messaging consistency with public positioning |
| Sales / customer success | Supplying verified traction and retention evidence |
| Security & compliance | Approving any trust/compliance claim before it reaches investor materials |

## Operating Cadence

Startup OS does not mandate a specific fundraising cadence. What's mandated regardless: the data room and core materials are kept current on an ongoing basis ([INVESTOR-DATA-ROOM.md](INVESTOR-DATA-ROOM.md)), and existing investors receive updates on a predictable rhythm ([INVESTOR-UPDATES.md](INVESTOR-UPDATES.md)) whether or not a raise is active.

## Review Expectations

Every artifact above that leaves the company — a deck sent to an investor, a diligence answer, an update — is reviewed for accuracy before it goes out, the same bar [MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md](../06-marketing/MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md) sets for public claims generally. A financial, legal, or compliance claim specifically routes through whoever holds that expertise (qualified advisors, not assumed internal knowledge) before it's stated to an investor.

## Decision Standards

Whether and when to raise, how much, and on what terms are decisions leadership makes deliberately — documented with the reasoning (see [FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md](FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md)), not decided reactively because an investor happened to reach out.

## Inputs and Outputs

- **Inputs:** business strategy and milestones, product roadmap, marketing narrative, sales/customer success traction, security/compliance posture, financial model.
- **Outputs:** a fundraising strategy, a pitch narrative and deck, a maintained data room, diligence responses, investor updates, and governance records.

## Scaling the System

At pre-seed/seed, with a founder doing this alone: the "data room" may be a shared folder, updates may be an occasional email, and governance may be informal advisor conversations. What must not shrink to zero: no claim goes to an investor that isn't true and verified, and nothing said to an investor contradicts what's said anywhere else. Formalize structure as the company and cap table grow.
