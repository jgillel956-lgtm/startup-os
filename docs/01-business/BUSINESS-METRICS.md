# Business Metrics

Defines practical business metrics: revenue, growth, retention, margin, burn, runway, pipeline, customer health, adoption, delivery progress, operating risk, and milestone achievement. Like every other metrics standard in Startup OS, these measure the business's actual health — not vanity numbers chosen because they look good — and no specific tool or accounting method is assumed.

## Revenue and Growth

Actual revenue and its growth rate over time — the same figures [INVESTOR-METRICS.md](../08-investors/INVESTOR-METRICS.md) reports to investors, reused directly rather than tracked separately for internal versus external purposes.

## Retention

Pulled from [CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-METRICS.md](../13-customer-success/CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-METRICS.md) (renewal rate, churn) — the single clearest signal of whether the business model's value creation ([BUSINESS-MODEL.md](BUSINESS-MODEL.md)) is actually holding up with real customers.

## Margin

Revenue against cost, reflecting the cost structure in [BUSINESS-MODEL.md](BUSINESS-MODEL.md) — the direct check on whether the business model is sustainable at scale, not just at current volume.

## Burn and Runway

From [FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md](../08-investors/FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md) — tracked continuously, since burn patterns shift and a runway calculated once goes stale fast.

## Pipeline

From [SALES-METRICS.md](../07-sales/SALES-METRICS.md) (qualified pipeline, conversion, win rate) — the forward-looking complement to trailing revenue numbers.

## Customer Health

From [CUSTOMER-HEALTH.md](../13-customer-success/CUSTOMER-HEALTH.md) — a portfolio-level view of account risk that current revenue alone doesn't show; strong current revenue with declining account health is a warning current numbers alone would miss.

## Adoption

From [ADOPTION-AND-ENGAGEMENT.md](../13-customer-success/ADOPTION-AND-ENGAGEMENT.md) — whether customers are actually realizing the value the business model assumes they are, which retention alone can lag behind reporting.

## Delivery Progress

From [ENGINEERING-METRICS.md](../03-engineering/ENGINEERING-METRICS.md) and [PRODUCT-LIFECYCLE.md](../02-product/PRODUCT-LIFECYCLE.md) milestones — whether the roadmap supporting strategy is actually advancing, not just planned.

## Operating Risk

From [BUSINESS-RISKS-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md](BUSINESS-RISKS-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md) and domain-level risk trends (security, technical debt, vendor risk) — read together at the business level, since a risk that's minor in one domain can be material combined with others.

## Milestone Achievement

Progress against the specific milestones in [GOALS-AND-OKRS.md](GOALS-AND-OKRS.md) and [FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md](../08-investors/FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md) — the concrete measure of whether strategy is translating into actual progress.

## Using These Metrics

- Review on [OPERATING-CADENCE.md](OPERATING-CADENCE.md)'s rhythm, feeding [STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md](STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md).
- React to trends across periods, not single data points — the same discipline every domain-level metrics standard already applies.
- These are the same numbers each domain already tracks — reused here at the company level, never recalculated into a separately-flattering version for leadership or investors.

## Right-Sizing for Team Size

At one founder: most of this is a short list, checked before any leadership decision or investor conversation. The metrics don't change as the company grows, only the rigor and tooling used to track them.
