# Business Model

Defines how the business model, revenue model, value creation, value capture, cost structure, pricing assumptions, and sustainability are documented. No specific business model, pricing model, or accounting method is assumed or mandated. This document does not provide accounting, tax, or financial advice.

## Value Creation

What the company actually creates for customers — grounded in the same problem/outcome evidence product and customer success use ([CAPABILITY-MODEL.md](../02-product/CAPABILITY-MODEL.md), [CUSTOMER-OUTCOMES.md](../13-customer-success/CUSTOMER-OUTCOMES.md)). A business model built on value the product doesn't actually deliver is a business model built on a false premise.

## Value Capture

How the company converts the value it creates into revenue — pricing structure, packaging, and the mechanism by which customers pay. Consistent with what's actually offered in [PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md](../07-sales/PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md), not a theoretical model disconnected from real deals.

## Revenue Model

The specific pattern of how revenue is generated and recognized — described plainly, without assuming a particular model. Whatever the model is, it's documented clearly enough that someone unfamiliar with the business could understand how money actually flows in.

## Cost Structure

The major cost drivers of the business, and how they scale (or don't) with growth — informs [FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md](../08-investors/FINANCIAL-MODEL-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md)'s unit economics rather than being reconstructed separately there.

## Pricing Assumptions

The assumptions behind current pricing — what the market will bear, what value justifies the price — stated explicitly so they can be tested and revisited, not treated as permanently fixed.

## Sustainability

Whether the business model, at scale, produces more value captured than value cost to deliver — the fundamental check any business model needs to eventually pass. Tracked via [BUSINESS-METRICS.md](BUSINESS-METRICS.md) (margin, unit economics) rather than assumed.

## Review

The business model is revisited when: pricing assumptions are repeatedly challenged in the market ([OBJECTION-HANDLING.md](../07-sales/OBJECTION-HANDLING.md) patterns), cost structure shifts materially, or growth reveals the model doesn't scale the way it was assumed to.

## Required Artifacts

A documented business model: value creation, value capture, revenue model, cost structure, pricing assumptions — kept current, not written once at founding and never revisited.

## Ownership

Leadership owns the business model description; finance (or the founder, at small scale) grounds it in actual cost and pricing data; sales and customer success supply real-world signal on whether pricing assumptions hold.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Model disconnected from actual deals.** A documented business model that doesn't match what sales is actually pricing and closing.
- **Unexamined pricing assumptions.** Pricing that's never been checked against actual market response.
- **Sustainability assumed, not verified.** Believing the model works at scale without checking unit economics against real data.
