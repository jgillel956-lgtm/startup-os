# Market and Customer Strategy

Defines how markets, customer segments, target customers, buyer needs, customer problems, and market assumptions are documented at the business-strategy level. This is the company-level anchor that marketing's [IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md](../06-marketing/IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md) and sales' [IDEAL-CUSTOMER-AND-QUALIFICATION.md](../07-sales/IDEAL-CUSTOMER-AND-QUALIFICATION.md) both derive from — one target customer, described consistently across all three.

## Markets

Which market(s) the company operates in and is deliberately choosing to focus on, per [COMPANY-STRATEGY.md](COMPANY-STRATEGY.md)'s market focus — with the reasoning for that focus stated, not just the choice.

## Customer Segments

The distinct groups of customers the company serves or intends to serve, each with its own characteristics — feeding directly into marketing's segments and sales' ICP so all three domains describe the same segments, not independently-invented ones.

## Target Customers

The specific customer profile the company is building for right now — grounded in real evidence (research, sales conversations, support patterns), not an idealized customer nobody's actually validated.

## Buyer Needs

What the actual decision-maker cares about when choosing a solution — distinct from user needs when the buyer and user differ (see [IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md](../06-marketing/IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md)'s buyer vs. user persona distinction).

## Customer Problems

The core problems the business exists to solve — the business-strategy statement of what product documents in detail per capability ([CAPABILITY-MODEL.md](../02-product/CAPABILITY-MODEL.md)). This document states the problem at company level; product documents it at the level of what's actually being built.

## Market Assumptions

Stated explicitly: market size, growth rate, competitive dynamics, willingness to pay — assumptions the strategy depends on, tracked in [BUSINESS-RISKS-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md](BUSINESS-RISKS-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md) since a wrong market assumption is a business risk, not just an interesting fact.

## Keeping This Grounded

This document is revisited whenever marketing's or sales' real-world signal contradicts it — a segment that isn't converting, a buyer need that turns out to be different than assumed. Business strategy doesn't dictate reality to marketing and sales; it's corrected by their evidence.

## Required Artifacts

A documented market and customer strategy: markets, segments, target customer, buyer needs, core problems, and market assumptions — consistent with marketing's ICP and sales' qualification criteria.

## Ownership

Leadership owns this strategy; marketing and sales supply the real-world evidence that keeps it accurate.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Three different target customers.** Business strategy, marketing's ICP, and sales' qualification criteria describing subtly different customers.
- **Unexamined market assumptions.** A market-size or growth assumption never checked against real evidence.
- **Strategy that ignores conversion signal.** Continuing to target a segment that consistently isn't converting, without revisiting why.
