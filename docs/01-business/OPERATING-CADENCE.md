# Operating Cadence

Defines the company operating rhythm: planning cycles, leadership reviews, and weekly/monthly/quarterly/annual reviews. No specific planning framework or tool is mandated.

## Why a Stated Cadence Matters

Without a stated rhythm, review happens whenever someone remembers to do it — which in practice means problems surface late and progress isn't checked consistently. A stated cadence, even a minimal one, is what makes [STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md](STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md) and [GOALS-AND-OKRS.md](GOALS-AND-OKRS.md) progress tracking actually happen.

## Planning Cycles

The rhythm at which goals are set or adjusted (commonly quarterly, sometimes annual for longer-range planning) — whatever the interval, it's consistent and known in advance, not decided ad hoc each time.

## Weekly Reviews

A short, frequent check on operational health — is anything urgent stuck, is the week's priority work actually happening. Lightweight by design; this is not where strategy gets revisited.

## Monthly Reviews

A broader check: [BUSINESS-METRICS.md](BUSINESS-METRICS.md) trends, progress against current goals, and any risk that's grown since the last review ([BUSINESS-RISKS-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md](BUSINESS-RISKS-AND-ASSUMPTIONS.md)).

## Quarterly Planning

Where goals are set or substantially revised for the next period, informed by the prior quarter's actual results — not a repeat of the same goals without checking whether they were met or still make sense.

## Annual Planning

Longer-range strategic direction ([COMPANY-STRATEGY.md](COMPANY-STRATEGY.md)) is revisited — checked against a full year of evidence, not just the most recent quarter's.

## Leadership Reviews

Distinct from domain-specific reviews (e.g. [PRODUCT-REVIEW-CHECKLIST.md](../02-product/PRODUCT-REVIEW-CHECKLIST.md), sales pipeline review) — a leadership review looks across domains for company-level signal, not just one domain's health.

## Required Artifacts

A stated cadence (which reviews happen when); a brief record per review — what was checked, what changed as a result.

## Ownership

Leadership owns the cadence and runs company-level reviews; each domain owner runs their own reviews on a compatible rhythm.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Cadence in name only.** A stated review schedule that gets skipped whenever things are busy — which is exactly when review matters most.
- **Review without consequence.** Reviews that happen but never actually change a goal, priority, or decision.
- **Mismatched rhythms.** Domain-level reviews happening on a schedule that never aligns with company-level review, so signal doesn't actually roll up.
