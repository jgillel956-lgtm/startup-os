# Goals and OKRs

Defines how company goals, objectives, key results, priorities, and milestones are created, tracked, and reviewed. No specific goal-setting framework is mandated — "OKRs" in the title names a common pattern; a company may use a different framework as long as the same discipline applies.

## Goals Trace to Strategy

Every goal connects to a stated strategic theme in [COMPANY-STRATEGY.md](COMPANY-STRATEGY.md) — a goal that doesn't trace to strategy is either revealing a strategy gap (something the company is prioritizing that strategy doesn't mention) or is a goal that shouldn't be a priority right now.

## Objectives and Key Results

An objective is qualitative — the outcome wanted. Key results are the specific, measurable signals that confirm the objective was actually achieved, not just worked on. A key result that's really just "ship X" (an output) rather than a measurable outcome isn't doing its job.

## Setting Priorities

Priorities compete for the same limited attention and resources across domains — the same discipline [PRIORITIZATION-STANDARD.md](../02-product/PRIORITIZATION-STANDARD.md) applies to product work, applied at the company level. Not everything can be a top priority; naming what isn't matters as much as naming what is.

## Milestones

Concrete, dated checkpoints toward a goal — the same milestones [FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md](../08-investors/FUNDRAISING-STRATEGY.md) references when sizing a raise and [INVESTOR-UPDATES.md](../08-investors/INVESTOR-UPDATES.md) reports progress against. One set of milestones, used consistently, not different versions for internal planning versus investor communication.

## Progress Reviews

Goals are checked against actual progress on the cadence [OPERATING-CADENCE.md](OPERATING-CADENCE.md) sets — using real data from [BUSINESS-METRICS.md](BUSINESS-METRICS.md) and each domain's own metrics, not a subjective sense of "things feel like they're going well."

## Required Artifacts

A goal set per planning period, each with its strategic connection stated; a progress record per review cycle.

## Ownership

Leadership sets and owns company-level goals; each domain owner sets their own goals that ladder up to them and is accountable for progress toward theirs.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Goals disconnected from strategy.** Chasing a metric or initiative that doesn't trace to any stated strategic theme.
- **Output dressed as outcome.** A key result that's really just "did the task," not "achieved the result."
- **Too many top priorities.** A goal set so long that nothing is actually prioritized over anything else.
