# Company Strategy

Defines how company mission, vision, strategic themes, market focus, customer focus, differentiation, and long-term direction are documented and reviewed.

## Mission and Vision

Mission is what the company does and for whom, now. Vision is the longer-term future state the company is working toward. Both are stated specifically enough to guide a real decision — a mission so broad it could apply to any company isn't doing its job.

## Strategic Themes

The small number of areas the company is deliberately focusing effort on for the current period — not everything the company could plausibly do, but what it's actually prioritizing. Strategic themes are what [GOALS-AND-OKRS.md](GOALS-AND-OKRS.md) and [PRIORITIZATION-STANDARD.md](../02-product/PRIORITIZATION-STANDARD.md) get evaluated against.

## Market Focus

Which market(s) the company is deliberately targeting, grounded in the same evidence [MARKET-AND-CUSTOMER-STRATEGY.md](MARKET-AND-CUSTOMER-STRATEGY.md) documents — not assumed, and not every market the product could theoretically serve.

## Customer Focus

Which customer segments the company prioritizes, consistent with marketing's [IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md](../06-marketing/IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md) — company strategy and marketing's ICP should describe the same target customer, not two different ones.

## Differentiation

What makes the company's approach distinct and defensible — the business-level version of [POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md](../06-marketing/POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md)'s differentiation claim and [PITCH-NARRATIVE.md](../08-investors/PITCH-NARRATIVE.md)'s moat section. All three should describe the same actual mechanism, not three different stories.

## Long-Term Direction

Where the company is trying to be in the longer term — specific enough to rule some things out, not so specific it can't adapt as evidence comes in.

## Documenting Strategy

Strategy is written down, not just held informally — a strategy that only exists in the founder's head can't be checked against, referenced by other domains, or handed off if the founder is unavailable.

## Review

Strategy is revisited on the cadence [OPERATING-CADENCE.md](OPERATING-CADENCE.md) sets, and whenever evidence materially contradicts it (see [STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md](STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md)) — not treated as fixed once written.

## Required Artifacts

A written strategy statement covering mission, vision, strategic themes, market/customer focus, and differentiation; a revision history showing when and why it changed.

## Ownership

Leadership owns company strategy; every domain owner should be able to explain how their work connects to it.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Strategy as slogan.** A mission/vision statement too vague to ever conflict with a real decision.
- **Strategy disconnected from evidence.** A strategic theme that ignores what product, sales, or customer data actually shows.
- **Undocumented strategy.** Direction that exists only in conversation, never written down where the company can reference or hold itself to it.
