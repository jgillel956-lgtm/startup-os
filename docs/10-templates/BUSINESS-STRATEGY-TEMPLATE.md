# Business Strategy Template

## Purpose

A fillable one-pager capturing company strategy — mission, vision, strategic themes, market/customer focus, and differentiation — in one place a whole company can reference.

## When to Use

At initial strategy-setting, and again whenever [STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md](../01-business/STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md) or [OPERATING-CADENCE.md](../01-business/OPERATING-CADENCE.md)'s planning cycle prompts a revision. Not a substitute for [COMPANY-STRATEGY.md](../01-business/COMPANY-STRATEGY.md)'s full standard — this is the fillable artifact that standard describes.

## Owner

Leadership, per [COMPANY-STRATEGY.md](../01-business/COMPANY-STRATEGY.md)'s ownership.

## Inputs

Prior strategy version (if any); recent evidence from [STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md](../01-business/STRATEGIC-REVIEW.md); market/customer evidence from [MARKET-AND-CUSTOMER-STRATEGY.md](../01-business/MARKET-AND-CUSTOMER-STRATEGY.md).

## Template Section

```markdown
# <Company> Strategy — <Date>

## Mission
<What we do, for whom, right now.>

## Vision
<The future state we're working toward.>

## Strategic Themes (this period)
1. <Theme> — why it matters now
2. <Theme> — why it matters now

## Market Focus
<Market(s) we're deliberately targeting, and why.>

## Customer Focus
<Target segment(s), consistent with marketing's ICP and sales' qualification criteria.>

## Differentiation
<What makes our approach distinct and defensible — the specific mechanism, not a superlative.>

## Not Doing (this period)
<What we're deliberately not prioritizing, and why.>

## Owner
<Owner> — <Date> — Next review: <Date>
```

## Review Checklist

- [ ] Every strategic theme has a stated reason it matters now.
- [ ] Market and customer focus match marketing's ICP and sales' qualification criteria.
- [ ] Differentiation names a specific mechanism, not a vague superlative.
- [ ] "Not doing" section is filled in, not skipped.
- [ ] No placeholder text remains.
