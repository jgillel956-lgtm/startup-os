# Customer Onboarding Template

## Purpose

The fillable onboarding plan [CUSTOMER-ONBOARDING.md](../13-customer-success/CUSTOMER-ONBOARDING.md) requires per customer — milestones, responsibilities, and risks in one place.

## When to Use

At kickoff, for every new customer moving from signed agreement to active usage — per [CUSTOMER-ONBOARDING.md](../13-customer-success/CUSTOMER-ONBOARDING.md).

## Owner

Customer success (or implementation), per [CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md](../13-customer-success/CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md).

## Inputs

The sales-to-customer handoff ([SALES-TO-CUSTOMER-HANDOFF.md](../07-sales/SALES-TO-CUSTOMER-HANDOFF.md)) — confirmed need, commitments made, stakeholders.

## Template Section

```markdown
# Onboarding: <Customer>

- **Owner:** <Owner>
- **Kickoff date:** <Date>

## Customer's Success Criteria
<What "successfully onboarded" looks like to them, from kickoff conversation.>

## Milestones
| Milestone | Owner | Target Date | Status |
|-----------|-------|--------------|--------|
| <milestone> | <Owner> | <Date> | <status> |

## Responsibilities
- **Us:** <...>
- **Customer:** <...>

## Risks
- <Risk> — <mitigation>

## Completion
<Date completed; how completion was confirmed against the customer's own success criteria — not just a checklist.>
```

## Review Checklist

- [ ] Success criteria are the customer's own words, not assumed.
- [ ] Every milestone has an owner and date.
- [ ] Customer-side responsibilities are stated, not just the company's.
- [ ] Completion is confirmed against outcomes, not just checklist steps.
- [ ] No placeholder text remains.
