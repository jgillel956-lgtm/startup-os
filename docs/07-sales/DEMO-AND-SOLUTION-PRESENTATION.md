# Demo and Solution Presentation

Defines how demos and presentations connect customer problems (from discovery) to relevant product capabilities, proof, implementation reality, and next steps. No specific demo or presentation tool is assumed.

## Start From Discovery, Not a Standard Deck

A demo built around [discovery findings](DISCOVERY-AND-NEEDS-ASSESSMENT.md) shows the prospect their own problem being solved, not a generic tour. Skip or minimize capabilities discovery didn't surface as relevant — a longer demo covering irrelevant ground is worse than a focused one, not more thorough.

## Connecting Problems to Capabilities

Map each demonstrated capability explicitly back to a need discovery uncovered — "you mentioned X; here's how this addresses it" — rather than assuming the connection is obvious to the prospect. Only demo what's actually built and available ([CAPABILITY-MODEL.md](../02-product/CAPABILITY-MODEL.md)); a roadmap item is described as a roadmap item, never presented as available today.

## Proof

Back claims made during the demo with real proof points ([POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md](../06-marketing/POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md)) — a comparable customer's result, a verifiable metric — rather than assertion alone.

## Implementation Reality

Address what adopting the product will actually require: setup, integration, timeline, and any dependency on the prospect's side — set honestly, not minimized to make closing easier now at the cost of a rocky onboarding later (see [SALES-TO-CUSTOMER-HANDOFF.md](SALES-TO-CUSTOMER-HANDOFF.md)). Technical or integration questions beyond what sales can confidently answer route to engineering rather than being guessed.

## Next Steps

Every demo ends with a specific, agreed next step and date — not a vague "I'll follow up." An unclear next step is how deals quietly stall between stages (see [SALES-PIPELINE.md](SALES-PIPELINE.md) deal hygiene).

## Required Artifacts

A demo plan referencing the specific discovery findings it addresses; a record of what was shown, what proof was used, and what next step was agreed.

## Review

Demo approach and messaging draw on marketing's [positioning and messaging](../06-marketing/POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md) and the product's actual [capabilities](../02-product/CAPABILITY-MODEL.md) — a demo that drifts from either (overselling, or using off-message language) is corrected the same way marketing content gets corrected in review.

## Ownership

Salesperson delivers the demo; product/engineering input is sought for anything technical beyond sales' own confident knowledge; marketing confirms messaging consistency when a demo script is standardized for reuse.

## Anti-Patterns

- **The standard deck.** Running the same generic demo regardless of what discovery found.
- **Overselling the roadmap.** Presenting an unshipped capability as available to close a deal faster.
- **No agreed next step.** Ending a strong demo with nothing specific scheduled, letting momentum die.
