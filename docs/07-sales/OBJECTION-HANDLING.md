# Objection Handling

Defines how sales documents, responds to, escalates, and learns from common objections around value, price, timing, trust, risk, competition, and implementation.

## Objection Categories

| Category | Typical form |
|----------|----------------|
| Value | "I'm not sure this is worth it" |
| Price | "It costs more than we expected / than an alternative" |
| Timing | "Not the right time" |
| Trust | "I'm not confident this will work / that you'll be around" |
| Risk | "What if this fails, or the data isn't secure" |
| Competition | "We're also looking at [alternative]" |
| Implementation | "This seems like a lot of effort to adopt" |

## Responding to Objections

- **Listen for the real concern first** — a stated objection is sometimes a proxy for a different underlying concern; responding to the surface objection without understanding the real one rarely lands.
- **Respond with substance, not deflection** — a price objection addressed by restating value (tied to the business impact from [discovery](DISCOVERY-AND-NEEDS-ASSESSMENT.md)) is stronger than simply discounting.
- **Trust and risk objections** get factual, verifiable answers — security/reliability claims are only ever what's actually true, per [TRUST-COMMUNICATIONS-AND-CLAIMS.md](../14-security-compliance/TRUST-COMMUNICATIONS-AND-CLAIMS.md) (the same discipline [MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md](../06-marketing/MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md) applies) — never softened or exaggerated to close faster.
- **Competitive objections** are addressed with differentiation, not disparagement (see [COMPETITIVE-SALES.md](COMPETITIVE-SALES.md)).

## Escalating Objections

- A technical trust/risk objection beyond sales' confident knowledge routes to engineering for an accurate answer, the same discipline as [PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md](PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md)'s technical assumptions.
- A pricing objection that seems to require a real exception routes through pricing approval ([PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md](PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md)), not an improvised discount in the moment.
- An objection that keeps recurring across many deals is escalated as a pattern (see below), not just handled deal by deal forever.

## Documenting Objections

Every material objection and how it was addressed is recorded — this is what makes objection handling improvable rather than reinvented every conversation.

## Learning From Objections

- A recurring objection about price may mean packaging or positioning needs revisiting — feed it to [PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md](PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md) or marketing's [positioning](../06-marketing/POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md), not just a better individual rebuttal.
- A recurring trust/risk objection about a specific real gap (e.g. a missing certification) is product/engineering input, not just a sales talking point to work around indefinitely.
- Recurring objections are reviewed alongside win/loss patterns ([COMPETITIVE-SALES.md](COMPETITIVE-SALES.md)) on the same cadence.

## Ownership

Salesperson handles objections in real time and documents them; sales owner reviews recurring patterns and routes them to the right function (pricing, marketing, product, engineering).

## Anti-Patterns

- **Overcoming instead of understanding.** Treating every objection as something to be argued past rather than genuinely addressed.
- **Discounting as the default response to price objections.** Reaching for a discount before checking whether the real objection is value, not price.
- **Never escalating patterns.** Handling the same objection well individually, deal after deal, without ever feeding it back to fix the root cause.
