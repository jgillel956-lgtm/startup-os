# Marketing Operating System

Defines the overall marketing model: principles, ownership, planning cadence, standards, and how marketing collaborates with product, sales, design, and leadership. Tool-agnostic — no specific CRM, marketing automation, or analytics platform is assumed.

## Principles

1. **Claims trace to truth.** Every claim about the product traces to a documented capability or a verified fact — never to what sounds compelling ([MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md](MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md)).
2. **Positioning before promotion.** Clear positioning ([POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md](POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md)) comes before any campaign or content push — promoting an unclear position just amplifies the confusion faster.
3. **Know who you're for, and who you're not.** A precise ICP ([IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md](IDEAL-CUSTOMER-PROFILE.md)) that excludes some people is more useful than a vague one that includes everyone.
4. **Consistency compounds.** Brand and voice ([BRAND-AND-VOICE.md](BRAND-AND-VOICE.md)) applied consistently build recognition over time; inconsistency resets that clock every time.
5. **Lightweight until it needs to not be.** Early on, marketing may be one person writing the website and a handful of posts — the discipline (accurate claims, clear positioning, consistent voice) matters more than channel breadth.

## Ownership

Marketing owns market narrative, positioning, messaging, audience education, and demand creation — see the ownership boundary in [README.md](README.md).

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|-----------------|
| Marketing owner (founder or delegate) | Positioning direction, brand integrity, campaign strategy, review sign-off |
| Marketer / content creator (human or AI agent) | Drafting content, campaigns, website copy, competitive material |
| Reviewer | Claim accuracy, brand consistency, compliance-sensitive language before publish ([MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md](MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md)) |

One person can hold all these roles early on — the responsibilities still apply.

## Planning Cadence

Startup OS does not mandate a specific planning cycle. What's mandated regardless of cadence: positioning is revisited when the product or market changes materially (not left stale for years), campaigns are planned with a stated goal and evaluated against it, and content has an owner who's accountable for keeping it accurate over time.

## Collaboration Model

- **With product** — marketing consumes capability and roadmap documentation ([CAPABILITY-MODEL.md](../02-product/CAPABILITY-MODEL.md), [PRIORITIZATION-STANDARD.md](../02-product/PRIORITIZATION-STANDARD.md)); it does not announce or promise capabilities product hasn't committed to.
- **With design** — marketing follows the [design system](../05-design/DESIGN-SYSTEM.md) and [brand-adjacent content standards](../05-design/CONTENT-AND-MICROCOPY.md) for anything customer-facing, so product and marketing surfaces feel like one company.
- **With sales** (future layer) — marketing hands off qualified interest with context ([MARKETING-TO-SALES-HANDOFF.md](MARKETING-TO-SALES-HANDOFF.md)); once a sales operating system exists, this becomes a two-way relationship.
- **With leadership** — marketing reports performance via [MARKETING-METRICS.md](MARKETING-METRICS.md) and escalates anything with legal, compliance, or reputational exposure through [MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md](MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md) rather than publishing and hoping.

## Inputs and Outputs

- **Inputs:** product capabilities and roadmap direction, design system and brand assets, customer/support themes ([CUSTOMER-FEEDBACK-LOOP.md](../09-operations/CUSTOMER-FEEDBACK-LOOP.md)), competitive intelligence.
- **Outputs:** positioning and messaging documents, website/landing pages, content, campaigns, competitive material, and qualified interest handed to sales.

## Scaling the System

At one marketer (or a founder doing double duty): positioning may be a page, campaigns may be a handful of posts, and review may be a self-check against [MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md](MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md)'s checklist. What must not shrink to zero: claims still trace to something true, and anything with legal/compliance exposure still gets a second look before publishing. Formalize as the team grows.
