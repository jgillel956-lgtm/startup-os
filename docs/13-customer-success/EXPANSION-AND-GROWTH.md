# Expansion and Growth

Defines how customer success identifies expansion opportunities, validates customer value, coordinates with sales, and avoids pushing growth before outcomes are proven. No specific CRM or expansion-tracking tool is assumed.

## Identifying Expansion Opportunities

Signals: usage approaching a plan/capacity limit, a stated need for a capability outside current scope, or a stakeholder describing a use case the current agreement doesn't cover. Expansion opportunities are noticed through genuine engagement with the account, not manufactured on a schedule.

## Validating Customer Value First

Per [CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md](CUSTOMER-SUCCESS-OPERATING-SYSTEM.md) principle 4: expansion is proposed only after the current scope has demonstrated real value ([CUSTOMER-OUTCOMES.md](CUSTOMER-OUTCOMES.md)). Proposing more before the current agreement has proven itself reads as a sales push rather than a genuine fit — and often is one.

## Coordinating With Sales

Once an expansion opportunity is validated, customer success hands the commercial motion to sales ([SALES-PIPELINE.md](../07-sales/SALES-PIPELINE.md)) with the same discipline as [RENEWALS-AND-RETENTION.md](RENEWALS-AND-RETENTION.md)'s handoff: account context, the specific need identified, and why now. Customer success does not run its own parallel pricing conversation.

## Avoiding Premature Growth Pushes

- Do not propose expansion to an account showing health risk — fix the relationship first (see [CUSTOMER-HEALTH.md](CUSTOMER-HEALTH.md)); an expansion pitch to a struggling account reads as tone-deaf and can accelerate churn.
- Do not propose expansion based on assumed need — validate with the customer directly (see [DISCOVERY-AND-NEEDS-ASSESSMENT.md](../07-sales/DISCOVERY-AND-NEEDS-ASSESSMENT.md)'s discipline, applied to an existing account instead of a new prospect).

## Required Artifacts

An expansion opportunity record: the signal that triggered it, the validated need, and the account's current health status at the time it was identified.

## Ownership

Customer success identifies and validates expansion opportunities; sales owns pricing, proposal, and close once handed off (see [README.md](README.md) ownership boundary).

## Anti-Patterns

- **Expansion quotas driving behavior.** Pushing upsell conversations to hit a number rather than because the account actually needs more.
- **Expanding an unhealthy account.** Proposing growth to an account that hasn't demonstrated value or is showing risk signals.
- **Customer success running its own deal.** Negotiating expansion terms directly instead of handing off to sales.
