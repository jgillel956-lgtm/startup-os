# Customer Outcomes

Defines how customer outcomes, success plans, business goals, measurable value, and customer proof points are documented.

## Outcomes vs. Usage

Usage ([ADOPTION-AND-ENGAGEMENT.md](ADOPTION-AND-ENGAGEMENT.md)) is a leading indicator; an outcome is the actual result the customer bought the product to achieve. A customer can be highly engaged and still not be seeing the outcome they expected — outcomes are checked directly, not inferred purely from usage.

## Success Plans

Built from the customer's own stated goals (captured at [onboarding](CUSTOMER-ONBOARDING.md)): what they're trying to achieve, by when, and how it will be measured. A success plan is specific to the customer — reusing a generic template without adapting it to their actual goals defeats the purpose.

## Business Goals

Tied to the customer's own business context, not the product's feature list — "reduce processing time by X" is a business goal; "use feature Y" is not, even if using feature Y is how the goal gets achieved.

## Measurable Value

Where possible, value is quantified in the customer's own terms (time saved, cost reduced, risk avoided) — a concrete number is more convincing internally to the customer's own stakeholders than a qualitative impression, and it's what [RENEWALS-AND-RETENTION.md](RENEWALS-AND-RETENTION.md)'s value reinforcement relies on.

## Customer Proof Points

A confirmed, quantified outcome — with the customer's actual permission to reference it — becomes a proof point for [marketing](../06-marketing/POSITIONING-AND-MESSAGING.md) and [sales](../07-sales/DEMO-AND-SOLUTION-PRESENTATION.md). Permission is confirmed explicitly before any external use, the same discipline as [MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md](../06-marketing/MARKETING-REVIEW-AND-APPROVAL.md)'s customer-reference check.

## Documenting Outcomes

Outcomes are recorded against the original success plan — did the customer achieve what they set out to achieve, partially, or not yet. An outcome record that only exists informally in conversation gets lost the moment the relationship changes hands.

## Required Artifacts

A success plan per account (or per segment, for smaller accounts where individual plans aren't practical); an outcomes record showing progress against it; confirmed permission before any outcome is used externally.

## Ownership

Customer success documents and tracks outcomes; marketing/sales request and use proof points only with confirmed customer permission.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Generic success plans.** A template applied without adapting it to what this customer actually said they wanted.
- **Outcomes assumed, not confirmed.** Assuming a customer is succeeding because they haven't complained.
- **Using a customer story without permission.** Referencing a customer's result externally without their explicit, current consent.
