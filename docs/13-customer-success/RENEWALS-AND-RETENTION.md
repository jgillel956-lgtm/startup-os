# Renewals and Retention

Defines renewal planning, retention risk management, value reinforcement, commercial coordination, and renewal handoff expectations. No specific CRM or renewal-tracking tool is assumed.

## Renewal Planning

Renewals are worked ahead of the renewal date — with enough lead time to address any health or relationship risk before the deadline forces a rushed conversation. A renewal date discovered a week out with unaddressed risk is a planning failure, not bad luck.

## Retention Risk Management

Retention risk is read from [customer health](CUSTOMER-HEALTH.md) and [relationship](RELATIONSHIP-MANAGEMENT.md) signals well before the renewal window — a renewal conversation should confirm a relationship that's already healthy, not be the first moment risk is addressed.

## Value Reinforcement

Ahead of renewal, the customer's actual outcomes ([CUSTOMER-OUTCOMES.md](CUSTOMER-OUTCOMES.md)) are reviewed with them explicitly — reminding a customer what they've gotten is more effective than assuming they've tracked it themselves. This is the renewal-specific instance of the ongoing value reinforcement [RELATIONSHIP-MANAGEMENT.md](RELATIONSHIP-MANAGEMENT.md)'s business reviews already do continuously.

## Commercial Coordination

Renewal terms, pricing changes, and contract mechanics are sales' domain ([PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md](../07-sales/PROPOSALS-AND-PRICING.md), [CONTRACTING-AND-CLOSE.md](../07-sales/CONTRACTING-AND-CLOSE.md)) — customer success supplies the readiness signal (is this account healthy, is this the right moment) rather than negotiating terms independently.

## Renewal Handoff Expectations

When a renewal needs sales' commercial involvement, the handoff back to sales includes: account health status, relationship context, and any risk or opportunity sales should know about — the mirror image of [SALES-TO-CUSTOMER-HANDOFF.md](../07-sales/SALES-TO-CUSTOMER-HANDOFF.md), flowing the other direction.

## Required Artifacts

A renewal timeline per account, started well ahead of the actual date; a value-reinforcement record ahead of the conversation; a handoff to sales when commercial negotiation is needed.

## Ownership

Customer success owns renewal readiness and timing; sales owns the actual commercial terms and negotiation once engaged.

## Anti-Patterns

- **Last-minute renewals.** Discovering an at-risk account only when the renewal date is imminent.
- **Assuming retention.** Not proactively reinforcing value, assuming a quiet account is a satisfied one.
- **Customer success negotiating terms.** Blurring into sales' commercial ownership instead of handing off cleanly.
